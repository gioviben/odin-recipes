Erbazzone Recipe: A Traditional Savory Pie from Emilia-Romagna

Home Page

Introduction

Erbazzone is a delicious savory pie from Reggio Emilia, in the Emilia-Romagna region of Italy. This rustic dish is made with a thin, crispy pastry filled with a rich mixture of Swiss chard (or spinach), Parmesan, and pancetta. Traditionally prepared by farmers to use fresh greens from the garden, erbazzone is a flavorful and satisfying dish, perfect as an appetizer, snack, or light meal.

Ingredients

For the Dough:

  • 250g (2 cups) all-purpose flour
  • 50ml (¼ cup) olive oil or lard
  • 100ml (½ cup) water
  • ½ teaspoon salt

For the Filling:

  • 500g (1 lb) Swiss chard (or spinach), washed and chopped
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 100g (3.5 oz) pancetta, diced (optional)
  • 100g (1 cup) grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil or lard
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

For Topping (Optional):

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil or melted lard
  • A sprinkle of coarse salt

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Prepare the Dough

  1. In a large bowl, mix the flour and salt.
  2. Add the olive oil (or lard) and water gradually, mixing until a smooth dough forms.
  3. Knead the dough for about 5 minutes, then cover with a cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.

Step 2: Prepare the Filling

  1. Heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil in a pan over medium heat.
  2. Add the diced pancetta (if using) and cook until crispy.
  3. Add the chopped onion and garlic, sautéing until soft and fragrant.
  4. Add the Swiss chard (or spinach) and cook until wilted and most of the liquid has evaporated.
  5. Remove from heat and let cool slightly.
  6. Stir in the grated Parmesan, seasoning with salt and black pepper to taste.

Step 3: Assemble the Erbazzone

  1. Preheat the oven to 180°C (350°F).
  2. Divide the dough into two parts: one slightly larger for the base and one smaller for the top.
  3. Roll out the larger portion into a thin sheet and place it in a greased baking pan.
  4. Spread the filling evenly over the dough.
  5. Roll out the second piece of dough and place it over the filling, sealing the edges.
  6. Prick the top with a fork to allow steam to escape.
  7. Brush with olive oil or melted lard and sprinkle with coarse salt.

Step 4: Bake the Erbazzone

  1. Bake for 30-35 minutes until golden brown and crispy.
  2. Let it cool slightly before slicing and serving.

Serving Suggestions

Erbazzone is best enjoyed warm or at room temperature, served with a side of cured meats like Prosciutto di Parma or a fresh salad. It pairs wonderfully with Lambrusco, a sparkling red wine from Emilia-Romagna.
